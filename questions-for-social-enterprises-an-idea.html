Questions for Social Enterprises - an idea

What do all NGOs/social enterprises have in common?

  • Their primary motive is to solve a problem in society - to create a positive change for everyone
  • In case of social enterprises
    • They have a sustainable financial model powering them
    • Since they are not optimized purely for profit, they cannot be competitive in a purely competitive marketplace and therefore they need external support. This support can be drawn from philanthropic funders/grants, the Moral Economy (a hazy community-powered marketplace regulation system) or Government Regulation.
    • When talking about grants or moral economy, they have to compete against other enterprises which spend more money on marketing/virtue signalling and not be adding much value.
  • In case of NGOs
    • They have a model to continuously market the work they are doing to funders and get operating funds.
    • They have to compete with other NGOs doing similar work to be able to raise funds.
  • How can a good NGO or Social Enterprise doing good work distinguish itself from others?
    • The current way is using the power of their marketing/sales channels and personal connects with funders (which inspires confidence).
    • There are some ways to solve this problem. Anuj Gupta and me have been talking about p2p review systems and transparency systems for doing this.
  • On the flipside, funders cant find the right orgs to fund often - there is too much noise and not enough signal.

The Idea

Can we make a model where the insights an organization has gathered in their domain of work becomes a signal about their authenticity?

This would create a marketplace where orgs can share domain-specific insights and answers about questions pertaining to solving social problems. In return, they will get access to people looking for orgs/experts in specific developmental areas - this can be location based, specialization based or .

Basically, a quora/stack overflow for social orgs working in the development space in India.

One way this can work and be useful

  • There are many ways to categorize Social Orgs (SOs) - location is one, size is another, one is by the problems they are trying to work on (even though they might have different solutions to the same problem)
    • Each SO can be listed as working under multiple problems - In particular NGOs, which cant survive by focussing on one problem, their funding needs to come for different types of projects for them to be resilient.
    • Once a problem becomes highly populated, it can be divided into subproblems and the SOs under that problem can be subdivided as well.
  • Example:

The problem: How to get everyone access to cheap drinking water in Villages? There might be a list of answers to this questions given by different SOs, which can be mapped out on a map or listed by some other category.

The answers to the above question can also lead to further questions, answers, and so on.

Some thoughts about implementation

  • There are only so many answers to the question "How to prevent violence against women". How to make sure that the first to answer the question doesnt get all the credit?
    • One way is to make sure if a question is solved by many SOs, it should be divided further into more detailed, related questions.
  • There should be some minimal proof of the solution being practical
