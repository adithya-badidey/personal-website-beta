Food and Sustainability in India

Why is food important to sustainabililty? Food is very important for human life and is almost central to sustainability. This is a module from apu-sus.

Finitude and Food

  • Post Independance, India couldn't produce enough food for itself and became dependant on US-imported wheat in the mid 60s.
  • This resulted in increased US control on Indian economic and international policies.
  • To get out of this problem, the indian government started investing in a set of interventions which is now called Green Revolution
  • Despite the advances in agriculture, India imports 25% of pulses (from Myanmar, canada, tanzania), oilseeds, etc
    • India is self-sufficient only wrt cereals

Fairness and Food

  • India has high rates of undernutrition despite high economic growth in recent years
    • How fair is economic growth?
    • Food self-sustainability =/= availability
    • 2 million in India do not have access to 3 steady meals a day throughout the year.
    • If we use UNFAO's criteria, 40% on India is estimated to be getting sub-optimal nutrition and calories
      • Not necessarily relevant to india's context because it doesnt take local situations in account
  • India's Public Distribution System is an important point. It tries to address the fairness in distribution of nutrition.

Undernutrition

  • In children, undernutrition leads to
    • Stunting
    • Wasting
    • Underweight children
    • Anaemic children
  • This can result to
    • Underdevelopment of mental and physical capacities
    • Increased economic and social fragility
  • Availability of nutritious food is also a problem
    • Many rural marketplaces dont have nutritious food on sale leading to a vicious cycle of undernutrition
  • Farming households face the same problem of nutrition as other households.
    • Farmers are net buyers of food
    • They may be buying things to get more diversity
    • They might be growing commercial crops
    • Even grain growing farmers buy grains off the market
  • Reduction of access to Commons Land has reduced their ability to forage
    • This reduces diversity of food consumed
  • We have moved away from more nutritious coarse cereals (like millets) to a more standardized foods
    • Availability
    • Prices
    • Cultural Norms and beliefs
    • Government policies
    • Ease of use/cooking

Food and Fragility

Our current system of agriculture is not sustainable.

Agriculture and Water Systems

  • India uses 90% of water withdrawal for irrigation when compared to US (36%), china (65%) and other countries
    • This is water which is not replenished into the land by the annual rainfall.
  • We use much more water for cultivation of specific crops compared to global averages
    • Even within india, the water use for different crops varies between areas.
    • Lots of factors affect water usage
    • Use of climate appropriate crops is essential
    • More than half of India faces groundwater depletion based water stressors
  • Water shortage leads to higher cost of cultivation
    • Water has to be bought at higher price or deeper borewells have to be dug
    • Shortage of water Increases uncertainity about agricultural output
    • This uncertainity increases social fragility and worsens argrarian distress
    • Farmers, unable to reliably earn from their land have been forced
      • To migrate for work
      • Farmer Suicides

Agriculture and Fertilizer Use

  • India uses more fertilizers /hectare than US (and other developed countries)
    • Beyond a threshold, higher application of inputs dont increase outputs
    • Fertilizers leach into groundwater and have turned up in drinking water
    • Farmers use fertilizers without protection and has lead to high prevalence of cancer and related diseases
  • Indian farming practices are input intensive and ecologically harmful
  • This has caused an impact on both Social Fragility and Ecological Fragility
  • Crop yields are affected by many factors, not just the inputs
    • Soil quality, agro-climatic zone, seasonal issues etc effect crop yields
    • India has generally lower yields

Organic Farming is an alternative to Industrial farming which might have better outcomes for sustainability.
