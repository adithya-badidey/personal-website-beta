Market shocks lead to changes in equilibrium

These are notes from MITxMicromasters program in Data, Economics and development policy.

Shifts in demand

An increase in demand leads to an outward shift in the demand curve

  • This leads to an increase in price and an increase in quantity as the equilibrium moves higher up the supply curve (which remains constant)

Shifts in supply

A decrease in supply leads to an inward shift in the demand curve (towards the price axis)

  • This leads to an increase in price and a decrease in quantity as the equilibrium moves higher up the demand curve (which remains constant)
