Radical Markets - Eric A. Posner, E. Glen Weyl

Authors: Glen Weyl and Eric Posner

The Premise

  • Ideal Market is the the best way to arrange a society
  • The problems with existing societal arrangement (Capitalism) and alternatives (leftist ideas) are:
    • Capitalism doesnt have free markets
    • Growth is stagnating under capitalism
    • Capitalism causes huge inequality
    • Central Planning is prone to corruption
    • The rise of the right in 21st Century

Context/Preface

  • Moral Economy has been replaced by a Market Economy
  • This book draws from the work of Adam Smith and radical economists like Henry George, one of the most widely read economists of all time.
    • Adam Smith originated the idea that if each person acts for their own personal good (while not harming others) it would lead to public good.
    • Henry George recognized that Private property is a monopoly and suggested a tax scheme that would create a Common Ownership structure.
  • William Spencer Vickrey was a follower of Henry George
    • He presented a paper call Counterspeculation, Auctions and Competitive Sealed Tenders - a paper by William Spencer Vickerey where he presented Auctions as a means to solve major societal problems.
    • Helped found the field of economics called Mechanism Design

Chapter 1 - Private Property is a monopoly

  • Allocation Efficiency and Investment Efficiency are important
  • Transaction costs lead to creation of Corporations because of Allocation Inefficiency
  • Corporations as a way to organize society
  • Henry George's Land tax
  • Role of government under Capitalism
  • Flaws of Central Planning
  • Coase's Theorem
  • Vickerey Commons
  • Texas Shootout
  • Partial Common Ownership retains Investment Efficiency
  • Common Ownership Self-assessed Tax (COST)

Chapter 2 - Radical Democracy

  • Democracy and the balance of power
  • Gridlock
  • Judiciary is the rule of elites
  • Jury theorem or the wisdom of the masses
  • Arrow's Impossiblity Theorem
  • Majority Cycling
  • Strategic Voting
  • Agenda Manipulation
  • Markets for collective decisions
  • Quadratic Voting
