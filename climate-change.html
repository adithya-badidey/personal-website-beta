Climate change

Having the right conditions is important for life systems to sustain. Any changes in temperature is likely to effect multiple systems and push them out of equilibrium. If systems are pushed beyond a Tipping Point, it leads to a change in the configuration of the system and can snowball into a larger problem

Why is increase in global mean temperature such a large problem?

  • Last 1000 years have been exceptionsal climate stability, with less than 1 degree of temperature variation
  • Global increase of 1 degrees is much more severe than local. It represents an immense amount of trapped energy/heat
  • It also means that local increases can be much more than 1 degree (overall mean of the planet has to be 1)
    • In several places, the seasonal variation has increased by much more
  • Impact on life on earth can be :
    • Rising sea levels
    • Lowered productivity of agriculture

What causes global warming?

  • Humans? Cant this be due to natural cycles?
    • There have been warm periods in the past which happen due to tilts in the earth's axis. These, however, happen over a period of millions of years. So our current problem is not due to natural cycles.
  • Greenhouse Effect - the trapping of reflected radiation inside the atmosphere. The right concentration of Greenhouses gases is the reason why the earth has a temperature which is conducive to live. Too little greenhouse gases would mean a cold planet (like mars), too much leads to Global Warming. The greenhouse gases are:
    • Carbon Dioxide
    • Nitrous Oxide
    • Methane
  • What releases these greenhouse gases?
    • Energy - renewable energy may help
    • Industrial Agriculture - organic farming might be the answer
    • Industrial Processes
    • Land-use and Forestry
    • ...

How are humans causing Climate Change

  • Burning Fossil Fuel for Energy
    • Electricity
    • Transport
  • Deforestation/Land-use change
  • Industrial Agriculture

Why should we act?

  • We should take the precautionary principle when dealing with an issue which has the potential to wipe out the human race.
    • This means not making it a close thing by acting soon.
  • Cost of not acting are very high compared to the cost of trying to fix the problem.

What prevents mass action in combating climate change?

  • Science is hard to interpret and understand
  • 80% of energy sources are fossil fuels and since Economy is driven by energy, Political consequences of controlling emissions are huge.
    • Evidence suggests that Energy access above a threshold has exponentially decreasing effect on Human Development Index
  • Example of a Wicked Problem
    • Many forces are at a head
  • Requires governments and individuals to act
    • Polycentric Governance
    • Governments are useless
    • Humans are shortsighted by nature.
  • Example of Tragedy of the commons - where we have a global commons
    • Local causes and global consequences

Kyoto Protocols

  • Developed countries have to meet emissions targets to combat climate change.
    • Because of historically high emmissions
    • Developing countries are exempt
  • Market for Carbon Credits

The way forward?

Mitigation

Direct reduction of carbon emissions

Adaptation

Learn to cope, prepare for effects for climate change

India's Commitments to Combating Climate Change

  • Paris Agreement - By 2030, reduce GHG emission intensity by 33-35% from the 2005 levels
    • Emission intensity is Emission per unit GDP
